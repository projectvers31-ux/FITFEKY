How Yoga Actually Burns Calories

While yoga isn't as intense as HIIT, it creates a calorie deficit through consistent practice. A 60-minute yoga session can burn 300-600 calories depending on intensity and body weight.

Week 1-2: Foundation Building

Start with basic poses: Mountain Pose, Tree Pose, Warrior I & II. Hold each for 30 seconds, repeat 3 times. Focus on breathing and balance.

Week 3-4: Building Strength

Add Chair Pose, Plank Pose, and Downward Dog. These engage multiple muscle groups and increase heart rate for better fat burning.

Week 5-6: Flow Sequences

Connect poses in Sun Salutation A & B. Flow through 5-10 rounds to build endurance and burn more calories.

Week 7-8: Advanced Integration

Incorporate Crow Pose, Headstand prep, and challenging balances. Your body will be more toned and flexible.

Yoga's Hormonal Benefits for Weight Loss

Yoga reduces cortisol (stress hormone) by 20-30%. High cortisol blocks fat loss. Regular practice improves sleep and reduces emotional eating.

Daily Morning Sequence (20 minutes)

  1. Mountain Pose - 1 minute
  2. Forward Fold - 30 seconds
  3. Plank to Chaturanga - 5 reps
  4. Upward Dog - 30 seconds
  5. Downward Dog - 1 minute
  6. Warrior I (both sides) - 1 minute each
  7. Tree Pose (both sides) - 30 seconds each
  8. Seated Meditation - 3 minutes

Evening Wind-Down Sequence

Child's Pose, Cat-Cow, Happy Baby, and Corpse Pose. This reduces cortisol and improves recovery for better fat loss.

"Yoga teaches you to listen to your body. This mindfulness leads to better food choices and sustainable weight loss."

Consistency is key. Practice 4-5 times per week, combine with clean eating, and track your measurements, not just the scale.
