Progressive Overload with Bodyweight

Building muscle without weights requires progressive overload. Increase reps, slow down movements, or add pauses to challenge your muscles.

Week 1-2: Foundation Building

Master basic form. Focus on controlled movements and full range of motion. Rest 60-90 seconds between sets.

Week 3-4: Increasing Volume

Add 2-3 reps per set. Slow eccentric (lowering) phase to 3-4 seconds. This builds strength and muscle.

Week 5-6: Advanced Techniques

Incorporate pauses at peak contraction (2-3 seconds). Add explosive concentric movements for power development.

Week 7-8: Peak Performance

Maximize reps while maintaining perfect form. Focus on mind-muscle connection for optimal growth.

Upper Body Muscle Building Circuit

Push Exercises:

  • Push-ups (standard, diamond, wide) - 3 sets of 8-15 reps
  • Pike Push-ups - 3 sets of 6-12 reps
  • Superman Holds - 3 sets of 20-30 seconds

Pull Exercises:

  • Inverted Rows (using table/bar) - 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Doorframe Pull-ups (assisted) - 3 sets of 5-10 reps
  • Superman Extensions - 3 sets of 10-15 reps

Lower Body Muscle Building Circuit

Squat Variations:

  • Bodyweight Squats - 4 sets of 15-25 reps
  • Pistol Squats (assisted) - 3 sets of 5-8 reps per leg
  • Wall Sit - 3 sets of 30-60 seconds

Lunge Variations:

  • Walking Lunges - 3 sets of 10-15 reps per leg
  • Reverse Lunges - 3 sets of 12-15 reps per leg
  • Curtsey Lunges - 3 sets of 10 reps per leg

Nutrition for Muscle Growth

Consume 1.6-2.2g of protein per kg of body weight daily. Focus on whole foods: chicken, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, and nuts.

Sample Daily Intake:

  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt with berries and nuts (40g protein)
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken salad with quinoa (50g protein)
  • Snack: Protein shake with banana (30g protein)
  • Dinner: Salmon with sweet potato and vegetables (45g protein)

Weekly Training Schedule

Day Focus Exercises
Monday Upper Body Push Push-ups, Pike Push-ups, Dips
Tuesday Lower Body Squats, Lunges, Calf Raises
Wednesday Rest or Light Cardio Walking, Yoga
Thursday Upper Body Pull Rows, Pull-ups, Face Pulls
Friday Full Body Burpees, Mountain Climbers, Planks
Saturday/Sunday Rest Recovery and Nutrition Focus

"Muscle building is 70% nutrition, 20% training, 10% recovery. Master all three for optimal results."

Track your progress with measurements and photos. Adjust your program every 4-6 weeks to continue progressing. Stay consistent and patient - results take time.
